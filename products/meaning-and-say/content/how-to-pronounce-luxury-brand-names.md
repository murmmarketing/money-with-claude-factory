# How to Pronounce Luxury Brand Names (Without the Awkward Pause)

There is a specific kind of hesitation that happens in front of a shop window or at a checkout: you know the brand, you love the bag, and you have absolutely no idea how to say the name out loud. You are not alone. Luxury houses are overwhelmingly French and Italian, and both languages break the rules English speakers expect — silent letters, pronounced final vowels, and stress that lands in surprising places.

Here is the short version, followed by the *why*, so the pronunciations actually stick.

## The five most-mangled names

- **Hermès** — say **air-MEZ**. The "H" is silent and the final "s" is voiced like a soft "z". It is never "her-meez" and never "hermes" like the Greek god.
- **Versace** — say **ver-SAH-chay**. Italian pronounces the final "e", so the name has three syllables, not two. Dropping the ending ("ver-SAHT-chee" or "ver-SACE") is the giveaway.
- **Givenchy** — say **zhee-vahn-SHEE**. The French "g" is soft, like the "s" in "measure".
- **Bvlgari** — say **BUL-guh-ree**. The stylised "V" is a classical Latin way of writing "U". So the logo reads "Bvlgari" but the word is simply "Bulgari".
- **Louis Vuitton** — say **LOO-ee vwee-TAWN**. The "s" in Louis is silent, and "Vuitton" glides: "vwee", not "voo-it-ton".

## Why French and Italian names trip us up

English speakers default to two habits that don't transfer: we go silent on final vowels, and we guess at stress. French does the opposite of the first — it *keeps* endings soft but present (the "-ent" in Yves Saint Laurent becomes a nasal "-awn"). Italian does the opposite of both — it pronounces almost every letter, including that final "e" in Versace and Moschino, and it keeps consonant clusters crisp (the "sch" in **Moschino** is a hard "k": mos-KEE-noh).

Once you know which language a house comes from, you can predict the pronunciation:

- **French houses** (Hermès, Givenchy, Louis Vuitton, Yves Saint Laurent): expect silent "h", soft "g", nasal endings, and stress that drifts to the end.
- **Italian houses** (Versace, Moschino, Bvlgari): expect every letter voiced, "sch" as a hard "k", and a pronounced final "e".

## Tech and auto brands have official answers

You don't have to guess with newer global brands — many have published the "correct" pronunciation themselves:

- **Nike** is **NY-kee** (two syllables, after the Greek goddess of victory).
- **Hyundai** is **HUN-day** — the U.S. campaign literally rhymed it with "Sunday".
- **Xiaomi** is **SHOW-me**, and **Huawei** is **WAH-way**, both confirmed by the companies.
- **Porsche** is **POR-shuh**, not "porsh" — the German name has two syllables.
