# The Rep Playbook — SEO Content

Beginner-intent SEO articles for **The Rep Playbook**, a one-off paid field manual for first-time rep buyers. Distributed via the MurmReps audience (SEO + newsletter), the free Chapter 1 lead magnet, and Reddit/Discord rep communities.

Each article targets a day-zero / early-buyer question, is written in an insider-but-welcoming tone (uses "W2C", "QC", "batch", "de minimis" but explains them), runs 600–1000 words, and closes with a natural **Playbook** CTA plus secondary **HaulHQ** (landed-cost tool) and **MurmReps** (finds) links.

**Positioning vs. HaulHQ (no overlap):** these articles answer *"how do I do this at all?"* (knowledge). They deliberately hand off cost/customs calculation to HaulHQ and sourcing to MurmReps rather than solving those in-line — complement, not cannibalize. All 2026 figures (US China de minimis ended 2025; shifting EU/UK VAT+duty thresholds) are framed as estimates, not tax/legal advice.

## CTA convention

Every article closes with:
1. Primary CTA to **The Rep Playbook** (`/`) — the paid field manual, referencing the relevant printable cheat-sheet (QC red-flags checklist, agent vetting scorecard, sizing reference, customs-by-country quick card).
2. Secondary links to **HaulHQ** (price the haul / landed cost) and **MurmReps** (vetted finds).

Keep CTAs plain and non-spammy — the brand voice is "trusted guide, not salesman."

## Disclaimer convention

Where customs/legality is discussed, articles state plainly that this is guidance, not tax/legal advice, that reps sit in a legal grey area varying by country, and that declaring imports is the buyer's responsibility.
