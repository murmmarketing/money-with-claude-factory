# How to Buy Reps for Beginners: A Complete 2026 Walkthrough

You saw a haul on TikTok, fell down a Reddit rabbit hole, and now you're staring at acronyms like W2C, QC, GL and batch with no idea where to start. This is the plain-English walkthrough nobody gave you. By the end you'll understand the whole journey — find, agent, order, QC, ship, arrive — and be ready to place a first order without torching money on a scam.

## Step 1: Understand the supply chain (30 seconds)

Reps are made in Chinese factories and listed on Chinese marketplaces — mainly **Taobao**, **Weidian** and **1688**. Those sites don't ship internationally and don't take foreign cards. That's why you need an **agent**: a middleman company that buys the item on your behalf, receives it at their warehouse, takes photos so you can inspect it (**QC**), then ships it to you. Everything else is detail.

## Step 2: Find something (the "W2C")

**W2C** means "where to cop" — a link to a specific seller's listing for a specific item. You don't guess or search random Chinese text; you use finds that other buyers have already vetted. Good sources:

- **Spreadsheets and finds sites** that catalog trusted listings by brand and item.
- **Reddit** communities (search the item name + "rep").
- **Product finder tools** that index tens of thousands of listings with prices and seller links.

A good find gives you the exact seller link, the price in Chinese yuan (CNY), and ideally recent QC pictures so you know the current batch is decent. Save the link — you'll paste it into your agent.

## Step 3: Pick an agent

An agent is your entire experience, so choose deliberately. The mainstream, beginner-friendly agents in 2026 (CNFans, Kakobuy, Superbuy, Sugargoo, ACBuy and similar) all do the same core job; they differ on **service fees**, **shipping-line access**, **warehouse photo quality**, and **app usability**.

For your first order, prioritize:

- **Clear QC photos** — you need to actually see what you're buying.
- **Good shipping-line selection** to your country.
- **A usable interface in English.**

Don't over-optimize on fees for order number one. Pick something popular with lots of tutorials, and learn the flow.

## Step 4: Place the order

1. Create an account and add funds to your agent balance (most take card or PayPal).
2. Paste your W2C link into the agent's "submit order" box.
3. Select the **size, color and variant** — this is where beginners slip. Rep sizing rarely matches your usual size, so check a sizing reference before you commit.
4. Confirm and pay. The agent now buys it from the seller.

The item ships from the seller to the **agent's warehouse** first (a few days), not to you.

## Step 5: QC — the most important step

When your item lands at the warehouse, the agent uploads **QC photos**. This is your one chance to inspect before it leaves China. Check logos, stitching, colorway, materials and overall shape against official retail photos. You then choose:

- **Approve** → it moves to shipping.
- **Refuse/RL (return-label)** → you reject it and either get a refund or a re-order (rules vary by agent).

Never rubber-stamp QC. Ten minutes here prevents a bad item you can't return once it's shipped.

## Step 6: Ship it home

Approve QC, then **submit the parcel for shipping**. The agent weighs and measures it, then offers **shipping lines** — different couriers with different price, speed and success profiles. Cheaper, slower lines exist; faster, pricier ones too. The number you're quoted often uses **volumetric weight** (size, not just mass) for bulky items like shoes, so a puffer jacket can "weigh" more than the scale says.

This is also where **customs** enters. Depending on your country's **de minimis** threshold and the declared value, you may owe VAT/duty on arrival. Plan the total landed cost before you ship, not after.

## Step 7: It arrives — legit-check and enjoy

When the package lands, inspect it again in person, compare against retail references one last time, and you're done. Keep your QC photos in case you ever need to compare batches later.

## The mistakes that cost beginners money

- **Skipping QC** to save time.
- **Guessing sizing** instead of checking a conversion chart.
- **Ignoring customs** until the bill arrives.
- **Chasing the absolute cheapest agent** before understanding the workflow.
