# Rep Slang Glossary: W2C, QC, GL, Batch and Every Term Decoded

The reps community talks in acronyms, and for a beginner it's a wall of jargon. This glossary decodes the terms you'll actually run into — organized so you can find what you need fast. Bookmark it and refer back whenever a thread loses you.

## The core five (learn these first)

- **W2C — "Where to cop."** A link to a specific seller listing for a specific item. When someone posts a fit and you ask "W2C?", you're asking for the source link.
- **Agent.** The middleman company (CNFans, Kakobuy, Superbuy, Sugargoo, ACBuy, etc.) that buys from Chinese marketplaces on your behalf, stores your item, takes QC photos and ships it to you.
- **QC — "Quality control."** The warehouse photos your agent takes so you can inspect the item before it ships. Also used as a verb: "QC looks clean."
- **Batch.** A production run of a rep. The same item can exist in multiple batches at different quality and price. Higher batches usually mean better accuracy.
- **Haul.** A group of items bought and shipped together — the thing people show off in "haul" videos.

## Buying and sourcing terms

- **Taobao / Weidian / 1688.** The Chinese marketplaces where reps are listed. You buy from these *through* an agent.
- **CNY / ¥.** Chinese yuan — the currency listings are priced in. Convert to your currency to gauge cost.
- **Seller / store.** The individual merchant on Taobao/Weidian who makes or sells the item.
- **Finds.** Vetted W2C links other buyers share; "finds spreadsheet" or a finds site is a curated catalog of them.
- **Rep vs. replica.** Same thing — an unofficial copy of a branded item.
- **Rep-worthy.** An item accurate enough to be worth buying as a rep.

## Quality and QC terms

- **GL — "Green light."** A trusted verdict that a specific item/batch has good QC and is safe to buy.
- **RL — "Return label."** Rejecting an item at QC and sending it back (for refund or re-order), rather than approving it to ship.
- **1:1.** Marketing shorthand for "identical to retail." Treat it skeptically — nothing is truly 1:1.
- **Flaw / defect.** A QC problem: loose stitching, wrong colorway, misaligned logo, stains.
- **Extra QC / additional pictures.** Requesting more warehouse photos when the first set is unclear.
- **PSP.** Extra services agents offer (e.g., adding/removing accessories, protective packing) before shipping.

## Sizing terms

- **TTS — "True to size."** The rep fits like your normal size (often it doesn't — check a chart).
- **Size up / size down.** Advice to order larger/smaller than usual because a factory runs small/large.
- **CM sizing.** Length in centimeters — the most reliable way to size shoes across factories.

## Shipping and customs terms

- **Warehouse.** The agent's facility where your items collect before you ship them home.
- **Consolidation.** Combining multiple items into one parcel to save on shipping.
- **Shipping line.** A specific courier/route your agent offers, each with its own price, speed and reliability.
- **Volumetric weight.** Billable weight based on a parcel's size, not just its mass — why bulky items cost more to ship.
- **De minimis.** The value threshold below which your country charges no import tax/duty. Ship over it and you may owe VAT/GST + duty.
- **Seized / seizure.** A parcel held or confiscated by customs. Rare, but the reason people watch declared value and thresholds.
- **Declared value.** The value written on the customs form for your parcel.

## Community terms

- **Fit / fit pic.** An outfit or a photo of one.
- **Deadd / dead add.** A duplicate or unnecessary item.
- **Legit check.** Asking the community to confirm whether an item looks accurate.
- **Cook / cooking.** Putting together a strong haul or find.
- **Slept on.** An underrated item worth buying.
