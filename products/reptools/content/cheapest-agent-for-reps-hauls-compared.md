# Cheapest Agent for Reps Hauls (2026)

"Which agent is cheapest?" is the most-asked question in every reps Discord, and the honest answer annoys everyone: **it depends on your haul.** The agent that's cheapest for a lightweight 2-item haul to Germany is often the *most expensive* for a bulky 8-item haul to the US. Anyone who tells you one agent always wins is guessing. This guide shows you how to actually compare — and why total landed cost is the only fair scoreboard.

## The three things agents compete on

Every buying agent — **CNFans, Kakobuy, Superbuy, Sugargoo, ACBuy** — differs on:

1. **Service fee** — a % of goods value (or per-order), 0%–5% in 2026
2. **Shipping line selection & pricing** — the base cost + per-kg rate for each carrier route
3. **Consolidation quality & extras** — repacking, box removal, QC photos, coupons

Most buyers fixate on #1 and ignore #2 and #3. That's backwards. On a typical haul, **shipping is a bigger line item than the service fee**, so a lean-fee agent with expensive lines can lose to a higher-fee agent with cheap lines.

## Service fees: the headline is a distraction

The 2026 reality is a race to 0% service fees. Several agents advertise **0% commission** to win signups — and it's real on the goods value. But agents still need margin, so they earn it on:

- **Shipping markup** (the per-kg rate you're quoted vs. their carrier cost)
- **Currency spread** on your wallet top-up
- **Value-added services** (extra QC, storage past the free window)

So "0% fee" is not the same as "cheapest." Read the shipping quote and the FX spread, not just the commission banner.

## Shipping lines: where the real spread lives

Two agents can resell the "same" carrier at different prices, and each curates a different **set of lines**. What you're really comparing:

- **Line availability** — does the agent offer an economy line to *your* zone?
- **Base weight cost** — matters most for small parcels
- **Per-kg rate** — matters most for heavy/bulky parcels
- **Volumetric divisor** — 5000 vs 6000 vs 8000 swings bulky-haul cost hugely
- **Sensitive-goods handling** — some lines refuse batteries/strong logos; sensitive-safe lines cost more

A practical pattern in 2026:
- Agents with strong **economy/sea-air lines** win on **heavy, bulky** hauls (shoes, jackets).
- Agents with lean **express base costs** win on **small, dense** hauls (accessories, a couple of tees).

Neither is universally cheaper. It's haul-shaped.

## How to actually compare: total landed cost

The only fair comparison holds the **haul constant** and recomputes the full landed cost per agent:

```
Landed cost per agent =
    goods (CNY × FX, incl. that agent's spread)
  + service fee
  + shipping (chargeable kg × that agent's line rate + base)
  + customs/VAT (same for all — depends on your country, not the agent)
```

Customs and VAT are identical across agents (they're set by your government), so the agent race is decided by **FX spread + service fee + shipping**. Rank those three summed, cheapest total wins.

## Worked comparison

Haul: ¥1,500 goods, chargeable weight 5.0 kg, shipping to the UK. Illustrative agent profiles (verify live rates — these move):

| Agent | Service fee | FX effective | Line per-kg | Base | Shipping | Landed (pre-customs) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Agent A (0% fee, pricey line) | 0% | 7.05 | $12.5 | $8 | $70.50 | ~$283 |
| Agent B (3% fee, lean line) | 3% | 7.15 | $9.0 | $6 | $51.00 | ~$267 |
| Agent C (0% fee, economy line) | 0% | 7.10 | $8.0 | $10 | $50.00 | ~$261 |

Goods ≈ ¥1,500 ÷ effective FX = ~$210–213 across agents. The **0%-fee Agent A finishes most expensive** because its line is pricey. Agent C wins by ~$22 on this bulky haul — but flip to a 1.2 kg accessory haul and Agent A's low base weight could put it first. Same three agents, opposite winner.

That $22 difference is one haul. Across a year of hauls, agent choice is easily $150–300 in your pocket or theirs.

## Things the price table won't show you

Cheapest isn't only about money:

- **QC photo quality** — some agents take lazy pics; bad QC costs you returns and reships.
- **Consolidation care** — an agent who removes shoe boxes and compresses saves you volumetric weight (real cash) that a lazy repacker won't.
- **Warehouse free-storage window** — how long items sit free before storage fees start.
- **Coupon stacking** — first-shipment and line coupons can beat a marginally cheaper base rate.
- **Payout reliability & support** — matters most when something goes wrong.

Weigh these against a small price gap. Saving $5 on shipping isn't worth an agent that ships you the wrong size with no photos.
