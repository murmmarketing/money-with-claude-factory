# How Much Does Agent Shipping Really Cost?

If you've assembled a haul, hit "submit parcel," and watched the shipping quote come back at double what you expected, you're not being scammed. You're being taught how agent shipping actually works. The price on your haul is never the price you pay, and the gap is almost always one thing: **volumetric weight**.

This guide breaks down every number that goes into an agent shipping quote so you can stop guessing and start estimating before you pay.

## The four things that decide your shipping cost

Every agent — CNFans, Kakobuy, Superbuy, Sugargoo, ACBuy — prices shipping on the same four inputs:

1. **Chargeable weight** (the bigger of actual vs. volumetric weight)
2. **The shipping line** you pick (each line has a base cost + per-kg rate)
3. **Your destination country** (lines are zoned)
4. **Consolidation** (how tightly your parcel is packed)

Get these four right and you can predict a quote within a few dollars. Ignore them and you'll overpay on every single haul.

## Volumetric weight: the number that gets everyone

Carriers don't just charge for how heavy your box is — they charge for how much *space* it takes up on a plane. A box full of down jackets weighs almost nothing but eats a huge amount of cargo volume, so carriers bill it as if it were heavy.

The formula every agent uses:

```
Volumetric weight (kg) = (Length × Width × Height in cm) ÷ divisor
```

The **divisor** is the whole game. Most air lines use **6000**, but faster/premium lines use **5000**, which makes light bulky parcels more expensive. Some economy sea-air lines use **8000**, which is friendlier to bulky hauls.

Worked example: a 40 × 30 × 25 cm box.

- Volume = 30,000 cm³
- At divisor 6000 → 5.0 kg volumetric
- At divisor 5000 → 6.0 kg volumetric

If your actual weight is only 3.5 kg, you get billed for 5.0 or 6.0 kg. That "extra" 1.5–2.5 kg is pure air, and at $9–14/kg it's real money.

**The rule:** chargeable weight = max(actual weight, volumetric weight). You always pay the higher one.

## Shipping lines: base cost + per-kg

A shipping "line" is a specific carrier route the agent resells. Each has two components:

- A **base/first-weight cost** (often covers the first 0.5–1 kg)
- A **per-kg rate** after that

Typical 2026 ranges to Western destinations (guidance, not quotes):

| Line type | Per-kg (USD) | Speed | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|
| Economy / sensitive-safe | $6–9 | 12–25 days | Heavy, bulky hauls |
| Standard express (EMS-style) | $9–13 | 8–15 days | Balanced hauls |
| Premium express (DHL/FedEx-style) | $13–20 | 5–9 days | Small, dense, urgent |

The counterintuitive truth: **premium express is often cheaper for small dense parcels** (low base weight, fast) but brutal for bulky ones (worse divisor). Economy lines flip that. There is no single "best line" — it depends on your haul's weight-to-volume ratio.

## Consolidation is free money

When your items arrive at the agent's warehouse, they sit in individual seller boxes stuffed with packaging. Before you ship, the agent **repacks** everything into one parcel — this is consolidation, and how aggressively they do it directly changes your volumetric weight.

Ways to cut chargeable weight at consolidation:

- **Request "remove shoe boxes"** — shoe boxes are the #1 volumetric killer. Shipping them adds 1–3 kg of air per pair.
- **Ask for vacuum/compression bags** on clothing hauls. Down jackets and hoodies compress dramatically.
- **Remove tags, dust bags, and extra packaging** you don't care about.
- **Consolidate more items into fewer parcels** — one big parcel spreads the base cost, but watch customs thresholds (see our de-minimis guide).

A good consolidation request can drop a 6.5 kg volumetric parcel to 4.5 kg. At $11/kg that's ~$22 saved per haul, every haul.

## The photo trap: "sensitive" surcharges

Some lines refuse or surcharge "sensitive" goods — batteries, liquids, strong brand logos, replicas flagged at customs-heavy destinations. Sensitive-safe lines cost more per kg but reduce seizure risk. If your haul includes anything electronic or heavily branded, factor the sensitive-line premium in rather than gambling on the cheapest line.

## Putting it together: a real estimate

Say you have a 5-item haul: 2 pairs of shoes (no boxes), 3 garments, packed to 42 × 32 × 28 cm, actual weight 4.2 kg.

1. Volume = 37,632 cm³ → at divisor 6000 = 6.27 kg volumetric
2. Chargeable weight = max(4.2, 6.27) = **6.27 kg**
3. Standard line at $10.50/kg + $6 base = **~$71 shipping**

Now request shoe-box removal and compression: pack drops to 38 × 30 × 22 cm.

1. Volume = 25,080 cm³ → 4.18 kg volumetric
2. Chargeable = max(4.2, 4.18) = **4.2 kg**
3. Same line = **~$50 shipping**

Same haul, **$21 saved**, purely from understanding the math.
