# Reps Landed Cost Calculator: The Full Breakdown (2026)

The number on the product page is a lie. Not a scam-lie — just an incomplete one. The ¥ price you see on CNFans or Kakobuy is maybe 55–70% of what the haul actually costs you delivered. The rest hides in FX spread, agent service fees, shipping, and customs/VAT. "Landed cost" is the only number that matters, and this guide shows you how to calculate it exactly.

## The landed cost formula

Every reps haul lands at:

```
Landed cost =
    (item prices in CNY × FX rate)
  + agent service fee
  + agent shipping (chargeable weight × line rate + base)
  + customs duty (if over duty de-minimis)
  + import VAT/GST
  + carrier handling fee
```

Six layers. Miss any one and your "cheap" haul quietly costs 40% more than you thought. Let's walk each.

## Layer 1: Item price + FX

Prices are listed in **CNY (¥)**. To convert:

```
USD ≈ CNY ÷ 7.2      EUR ≈ CNY ÷ 7.8      GBP ≈ CNY ÷ 9.1
```

(Rates drift — always use a current mid-market rate.) The catch: your **payment method adds a spread**. PayPal, card processors, and agent wallets often bake in 2–4% over the mid-market rate. A ¥1,500 haul at a "clean" 7.2 is ~$208; at an effective 6.95 after fees it's ~$216. Small per haul, real over a year. Always calculate FX at your *actual* effective rate, not the Google rate.

## Layer 2: Agent service fee

Agents make money on a **service fee** — a percentage of your order value, charged when you pay or when you ship. 2026 norms:

- Most agents: **0%–5%** service fee on goods value (many advertise 0% to compete, then earn on shipping margin)
- Some charge per-order or per-item handling instead

The 0% headline is real but read the shipping markup — a "0% fee" agent with a fat per-kg rate can cost more than a "5% fee" agent with lean lines. This is exactly why you compare *total* landed cost, not any single fee.

## Layer 3: Shipping (the volumetric trap)

Shipping is priced on **chargeable weight = max(actual, volumetric)** where:

```
Volumetric kg = (L × W × H cm) ÷ 6000
```

Then: `shipping = base cost + (chargeable kg × per-kg rate)`. Per-kg runs ~$6–20 depending on line and destination. Because bulky hauls get billed for air, this is the layer with the most room to save (remove shoe boxes, compress garments). See our dedicated agent-shipping guide for the full breakdown.

## Layer 4–6: Customs, VAT, handling

This is where US, UK and EU sharply diverge.

### 🇺🇸 US
The old $800 de minimis exemption **no longer shields China-origin parcels** (ended 2025). Budget for duty (category-dependent, footwear/apparel are high) plus fees regardless of haul size. US landed cost is now noticeably higher than the "under $800 = free" era buyers remember.

### 🇬🇧 UK
- Under **£135**: 20% VAT collected at sale, no duty. Add a ~£8–12 handling fee if collected at import.
- Over **£135**: 20% VAT + duty (category-dependent) + handling fee.

### 🇪🇺 EU
- **VAT on everything** (no de-minimis): 19%–27% depending on country.
- **Duty-free under €150** declared value; duty applies above.
- Plus an €8–20 carrier handling fee.

## Worked example: the same haul, three countries

Haul: 4 items, ¥1,600 total. Packed to 4.5 kg chargeable. Agent: 3% fee, line at $10/kg + $6 base.

**Shared costs:**
- Goods: ¥1,600 ÷ 7.2 = **$222**
- Service fee 3% = **$6.66**
- Shipping: (4.5 × $10) + $6 = **$51**
- Subtotal before customs = **$279.66**

**🇺🇸 US** (assume ~12% effective duty + fees on goods):
- Duty ~$27 + handling ~$0 (bundled) → **≈ $306 landed**

**🇬🇧 UK** (declared value ~£195, over £135):
- VAT 20% on (goods+shipping ≈ £245) = £49 + duty ~£23 + handling £10 → **≈ $383 landed**

**🇪🇺 EU / Germany** (declared ~€260, over €150):
- VAT 19% on €260 = €49.40 + duty ~€31 + handling €12 → **≈ $370 landed**

Same haul, but landed cost swings **$306 → $383** depending purely on destination rules. If those EU/UK hauls were split to stay under the duty threshold, you'd shave $30–40 off. This is why landed cost — not sticker price — is the only honest way to compare hauls.
