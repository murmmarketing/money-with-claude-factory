# Resume Bullet Points That Actually Get Interviews (With Examples)

Most resume bullets read like a job description: a list of duties nobody asked
about. Recruiters skim right past them. The bullets that win interviews do one
thing differently — they show **impact**, with a number attached.

Here's the formula and the examples to steal.

## The formula: Action + Task + Result

Every strong bullet has three parts:

1. **Action verb** — Led, Built, Reduced, Increased, Delivered, Automated.
2. **What you did** — the specific task or project.
3. **Measurable result** — a percentage, dollar amount, count, or time saved.

> **Led** a website replatform across 6 teams, **launching on schedule** with **zero
> critical defects**.

If you can't measure it precisely, estimate honestly ("~", "roughly") or use a
proxy metric like frequency, scale, or rank.

## Before and after

**Before:** *Responsible for customer support tickets.*
**After:** *Resolved 60+ support tickets per day across chat and email while
maintaining a 96% CSAT score.*

**Before:** *Helped with the company budget.*
**After:** *Led annual budgeting across 5 departments, delivering forecasts within
3% of actuals.*

**Before:** *Wrote code for the web app.*
**After:** *Built a React feature that lifted checkout conversion 12%, adding an
estimated $400K in annual revenue.*

See the pattern? Same work — but the "after" versions prove you were good at it.

## Lead with the right verbs

Start bullets with strong, varied action verbs. Avoid opening three bullets in a
row with "Responsible for" or "Managed." A useful bank to pull from:

> Achieved, Led, Built, Reduced, Increased, Delivered, Designed, Improved,
> Streamlined, Coordinated, Resolved, Trained, Launched, Owned, Drove, Optimized,
> Implemented, Negotiated, Analyzed, Automated, Mentored, Exceeded.

## Tailor the top third to each job

Recruiters spend most of their time on the top third of your resume. Reorder your
bullets so the ones matching *this* job's keywords sit at the top. You don't need
to rewrite everything for every application — just resequence and swap a few.

## Match your seniority

- **Entry-level:** lean on internships, coursework, and capstone projects. Quantify
  scope ("600+ supervised hours", "50+ calls per day").
- **Mid-level:** emphasize ownership and outcomes you drove directly.
- **Senior:** show leadership, cross-team impact, and business results.
