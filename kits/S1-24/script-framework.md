# The Explainer-Script Framework

A great explainer script is not a description — it's a 4-beat story told in the viewer's language,
timed to the second, with visual directions so the editor never guesses.

## The timing math (memorize this)
- Comfortable narration ≈ **150 words per minute** (2.5 words/second). Slightly slower for
  emphasis-heavy VO.
- 60 seconds ≈ **145–155 words**. 90 seconds ≈ **215–230 words**. Never write to the max — leave
  air for pauses, animation beats, and the logo sting.
- **Count your words before delivery.** A script that runs long is the #1 complaint editors have.

## The 4-beat arc
1. **HOOK / PROBLEM (0–15s):** Open in the viewer's pain, in their words. No "Introducing…", no
   company name yet. Make them nod "yes, that's me."
2. **SOLUTION (15–45s):** Introduce the product as the answer. Show the *one* core mechanism —
   not every feature. "Here's how it works" in 2–3 simple steps.
3. **PROOF / BENEFIT (45–70s):** The payoff — the outcome, the "so now you can…". Add a proof
   point if it exists (a number, a customer type, a result).
4. **CTA (last 5–10s):** One clear action. "Start free at [url]." Never two CTAs.

## The format editors love (two columns)
Write every script as a table so the editor can storyboard directly:

| # | VOICEOVER (what's said) | ON-SCREEN (what's shown) | ~sec |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | "You've got 12 tabs open and still can't find that one link." | Messy browser, tabs multiplying, frustrated user | 0–6 |

- **VOICEOVER** = exact words to record.
- **ON-SCREEN** = the visual/animation direction (UI shots, metaphors, text callouts).
- **~sec** = running timestamp so length is obvious at a glance.

## Writing rules
- **One idea per sentence.** Short sentences read better and cut cleaner.
- **Second person.** "You" and "your", never "users" or "customers".
- **Benefit over feature.** "Never lose a link again" beats "bookmark sync engine".
- **Name the product once early, once at CTA.** Not every line.
- **Read it aloud with a stopwatch.** If you stumble, the editor's VO artist will too.

## Delivery package (what you hand over)
1. The two-column script (this is the deliverable).
2. A one-line **tone note** (e.g. "warm, slightly playful, confident — think Notion, not a bank").
3. A **word count + target length** confirmation ("218 words ≈ 88s at 150 wpm").
4. One alternate hook line (gives the client a cheap choice and reduces revision rounds).

## Selling the volume deal to editors
Editors bill clients for the whole video; the script is a line item they'd rather outsource. Offer:
"Send me your script-less clients — 48h turnaround, you keep your markup, I stay behind the scenes."
Five scripts/month at $250 each = predictable $1,250/mo with zero client-finding on your side.
